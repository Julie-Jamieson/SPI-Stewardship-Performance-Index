# Stewardship Performance Index (SPI)

*A standardized framework for measuring, reporting, and improving long-term stewardship across infrastructure and industry.*

---

## Overview

The Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) provides a standardized framework for measuring, reporting, and improving long-term stewardship throughout the lifecycle of infrastructure and development projects.

Infrastructure and development projects influence the natural environment, surrounding communities, and the systems that support them long after construction is complete. Understanding and evaluating those relationships over time is the purpose of SPI.

For the purposes of SPI, stewardship refers to the ongoing measurement, maintenance, and improvement of a project's relationship with the natural environment, surrounding communities, and the systems that support them.

SPI is founded on a simple principle: **stewardship should be measurable.**

The framework establishes a consistent methodology for evaluating performance through objective indicators, transparent reporting, independent verification, and continuous improvement.

SPI is designed to complement existing environmental, regulatory, and sustainability reporting systems rather than replace them.

---

## Purpose

While many existing reporting systems focus on regulatory compliance, disclosure, or sustainability outcomes, SPI establishes a consistent methodology for evaluating long-term stewardship using objective indicators, evidence requirements, transparent reporting, and independent verification.

The framework is designed to be adaptable across sectors while maintaining a consistent assessment methodology.

The objective of SPI is to establish a common approach for evaluating long-term stewardship throughout the lifecycle of infrastructure and development projects.

---

## Core Principles

SPI is founded upon seven guiding principles.

* Stewardship is continuous.
* Stewardship should be measurable.
* Stewardship should be sustained through ongoing measurement, maintenance, and improvement.
* Environmental systems have value.
* Communities matter.
* Transparency builds trust.
* Independent verification strengthens credibility.

---

## The Five Pillars

SPI evaluates projects across five interconnected pillars.

* Water
* Energy
* Ecology
* Community
* Transparency

Each pillar contains measurable indicators, evidence requirements, assessment methodologies, and scoring criteria designed to support consistent evaluation.
