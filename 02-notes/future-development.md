# Future Development

This document captures concepts, governance models, methodologies, and implementation ideas that may influence future versions of the Stewardship Performance Index (SPI).

The contents of this document should be considered working concepts rather than requirements of the SPI Standard.

---

# Governance

## Potential SPI Governance Model

A potential governance model for SPI is to separate stewardship assessment from stewardship publication.

Under this model:

- Infrastructure operators provide the operational data, supporting documentation, and evidence required by the SPI Standard.
- Independent assessment organizations apply the SPI Standard and prepare or independently verify stewardship assessments.
- SPI provides the public platform through which verified stewardship assessments are published, maintained, and made accessible over time.

This approach separates measurement, verification, and publication while establishing a consistent public stewardship record that extends beyond ownership changes, election cycles, and organizational turnover.

Potential platform features may include:

- Public stewardship dashboards
- Historical assessment records
- Five pillar scores
- Overall SPI rating
- Long-term trend reporting
- Evidence summaries
- Independent verification statements
- Annual stewardship reports

This governance model remains under development and should be considered a future implementation concept rather than a current requirement of the SPI Standard.

# Methodology

## Cross-Pillar Assessment

Infrastructure systems are interconnected. A single project activity may influence multiple aspects of stewardship simultaneously.

SPI may therefore permit an indicator to contribute to more than one pillar, provided each assessment evaluates a distinct aspect of stewardship and avoids double-counting.

For example:

- Waste heat recovery may contribute to the Energy pillar by demonstrating efficient resource utilization.
- The same system may also contribute to the Ecology pillar if thermal discharge affects surrounding ecosystems.
- Community impacts resulting from heat, noise, or other operational characteristics may be evaluated independently within the Community pillar.

Cross-pillar assessment recognizes the interconnected nature of infrastructure while preserving the integrity of individual pillar assessments.

# Platform

## Public Stewardship Platform

SPI may provide a centralized public platform that publishes verified stewardship assessments prepared by independent organizations.

Potential features may include:

- Project profiles
- Interactive stewardship dashboards
- Historical performance trends
- Five pillar scoring
- Overall stewardship ratings
- Annual reports
- Evidence summaries
- Independent verification statements
- Downloadable assessment reports
- Public change history

The platform would function as the public stewardship record for participating projects.
