# Core Principles

The Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) is founded upon a set of guiding principles that inform the development of the framework, its methodology, and its application across sectors.

These principles establish the philosophy of SPI. They are intended to provide a consistent foundation for stewardship assessment regardless of project type, industry, or location.

---

## 1. Stewardship is Continuous

Stewardship extends throughout the entire lifecycle of a project. It begins during planning, continues through construction and operation, and remains relevant as infrastructure evolves, is renewed, or is ultimately decommissioned.

---

## 2. Stewardship is Measurable

Meaningful stewardship should be supported by objective indicators, evidence, and transparent reporting wherever practical. While not every aspect of stewardship can be reduced to a single metric, performance should be capable of consistent evaluation over time.

---

## 3. Stewardship Should Be Sustained

Stewardship is not defined by a single assessment or achievement. Long-term stewardship requires ongoing measurement, maintenance, adaptation, and improvement throughout the life of a project.

---

## 4. Environmental Systems Have Value

Natural systems provide essential ecological services that support communities, infrastructure, and future development. Stewardship should recognize, protect, and where possible enhance these systems.

---

## 5. Communities Matter

Infrastructure exists within communities and should contribute positively to the places in which it operates. Stewardship includes meaningful engagement, accountability, and consideration of long-term community wellbeing.

---

## 6. Transparency Enables Accountability

Stewardship should be supported by objective evidence, publicly available reporting, and consistent assessment methodologies that allow performance to be understood, verified, and evaluated over time.

---

## 7. Independent Verification Strengthens Credibility

Objective assessment improves confidence in reported outcomes. Wherever appropriate, stewardship reporting should be prepared or independently verified using consistent methodologies and evidence.

---

These principles guide the development and application of every component of the Stewardship Performance Index.
