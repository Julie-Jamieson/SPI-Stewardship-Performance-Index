# Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) Standard

**Status:** Working Draft

---

## Purpose

The Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) Standard establishes a consistent methodology for evaluating long-term stewardship across infrastructure and industry.

The standard defines the principles, assessment methodology, indicators, evidence requirements, reporting expectations, and governance framework used to evaluate stewardship throughout the lifecycle of a project.

SPI is intended to complement existing environmental, regulatory, engineering, and sustainability frameworks by providing a common methodology for measuring, reporting, and improving long-term stewardship.

---

## Scope

This standard applies to infrastructure and development projects whose activities influence the natural environment, surrounding communities, and the systems that support them.

The framework is intended to be adaptable across sectors while maintaining a consistent assessment methodology.

---

## Structure of the Standard

The Stewardship Performance Index Standard is currently organized into the following sections.

1. Introduction
2. Core Principles
3. The Five Pillars
4. Assessment Methodology
5. Indicators
6. Evidence Requirements
7. Cross-Pillar Assessment
8. Reporting Framework
9. Independent Verification
10. Governance
11. Continuous Improvement
12. Appendices

## Development Status

This document represents the working draft of the Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) Standard.

The structure of the standard has been established. Individual sections will continue to evolve through research, practical application, stakeholder engagement, and ongoing refinement.
