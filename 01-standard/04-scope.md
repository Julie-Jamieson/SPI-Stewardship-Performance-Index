# Scope

The Stewardship Performance Index (SPI) is intended to provide a consistent framework for evaluating long-term stewardship across infrastructure and industry.

SPI is designed to complement existing environmental, engineering, regulatory, and sustainability frameworks by providing a common methodology for evaluating stewardship throughout the lifecycle of a project.

---

# Intended Application

SPI may be applied to a wide range of infrastructure and development projects, including but not limited to:

- Artificial intelligence infrastructure
- Data centres
- Energy generation and distribution
- Manufacturing facilities
- Transportation infrastructure
- Industrial developments
- Water and wastewater infrastructure
- Research facilities
- Public infrastructure
- Large commercial developments

The framework is intended to remain adaptable as new industries and technologies emerge.

---

# Lifecycle

SPI considers stewardship throughout the lifecycle of a project.

This includes:

- Planning
- Approval
- Construction
- Operation
- Renewal and modernization
- Decommissioning, where applicable

Stewardship is evaluated as an ongoing process rather than a single point in time.

---

# Framework Objectives

SPI seeks to provide a consistent approach to:

- Long-term stewardship evaluation
- Transparent reporting
- Independent verification
- Continuous improvement
- Public accountability

The framework is intended to support infrastructure operators, municipalities, governments, Indigenous communities, researchers, investors, and the public by providing a common language for discussing stewardship.

---

# Relationship to Other Frameworks

SPI does not replace environmental regulations, engineering standards, environmental assessments, sustainability reporting, or other regulatory requirements.

Instead, SPI is intended to complement existing frameworks by providing a consistent methodology for evaluating long-term stewardship.

---

# Future Development

Future versions of the SPI Standard may include sector-specific guidance, implementation recommendations, and additional methodologies while maintaining a consistent stewardship framework.
